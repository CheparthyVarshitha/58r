The Bamboo Tree      The Elephant Rope      The Cracked Pot      The Lazy Student

The Bamboo Tree

A man planted a bamboo seed and watered it every day. For five years, nothing grew above the soil. People laughed at him, but he didn’t give up. In the sixth year, the bamboo shot up 80 feet in just six weeks. The truth is, the bamboo wasn’t inactive; it was growing strong roots. Moral: Be patient. Great things take time and deep preparation.

The Elephant Rope

A man saw huge elephants tied with small ropes and asked the trainer why they didn’t break free. The trainer said, “When they were young, they couldn’t break the rope. Now they believe they still can’t.” Just like those elephants, we often limit ourselves based on past failures. Moral: Never let old fears stop you from trying again.

The Cracked Pot

A water carrier had two pots. One was perfect, the other had a crack. Every day, the cracked pot spilled water on the path and felt ashamed. One day, the carrier said, “Look at the flowers on your side of the path — I planted seeds, and your leak watered them every day.” Moral: Our flaws can also be our strengths. Everything has value.

The Lazy Student

A student always failed in school and thought he was useless. One teacher gave him a chance, encouraged him, and made learning fun. Slowly, the boy improved, and years later, he became a successful engineer. He returned to thank his teacher for believing in him. Moral: Sometimes, one person’s belief in us can change our whole life. Believe in yourself, even if others don’t.
